Black Tail

The Blacktail deer that occupy coastal woodlands in the Pacific Northwest of North America are subspecies of the mule deer. They have sometimes been treated as a species, but virtually all recent authorities maintain they are subspecies. The Sitka Blacktail deer is found coastally in British Columbia, southeast Alaska, and southcentral Alaska.
